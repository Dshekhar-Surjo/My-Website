About § I
Between Frames
& Data Points
Shekhar Dash  ·  Chapel Hill, NC

Filmmaker, educator, and researcher — Shekhar Dash occupies a rare intersection where cinematic storytelling mee,ts learning science and data analytics. Born in Bangladesh and trained at Santiniketan; Tagore's university, and later in the Satyajit Ray Film & Television Institute, he spent nearly a decade building media institutions in Dhaka before crossing the ocean to pursue a graduate degree in Educational Innovation, Technology & Entrepreneurship at UNC Chapel Hill.


Today, he works as a Research Assistant at UNC's Mathematics Potential Lab, studies how AI and XR reshape learning, and work as an analyst for a VR athlete training startup — all while continuing to write screenplays and mentor the next generation of filmmakers through Payntara, his youth arts initiative.

"I don't switch hats — I wear them all at once. Cinema taught me how people perceive. Research taught me how to measure it."

Research & Current Work § III
Mathematics Potential Lab
Research Assistant  ·  UNC Chapel Hill  ·  2025–Present

Contributes to research on embodied, AI-supported learning, analyzing how movement and interaction shape mathematical thinking. Works with multimodal data — video, behavioral traces — using R and qualitative methods.

VER Coaching — VR Athlete Training
Analyst & Designer Intern  ·  Chapel Hill  ·  2025–Present

Analyzes and visualizes VR performance data using R to support coaches' decision-making. Edits and optimizes immersive video content to enhance feedback and learning within VR training environments.

Ongoing Graduate Research
From Instruction to Interaction: Designing an Embodied, AI-Supported Learning Ecology

An ongoing project exploring how AI- and XR-mediated, embodied interactions can transform classroom learning. Integrates design-based research, learning analytics, and interaction analysis. MEITE Program  ·  UNC Chapel Hill  ·  2025–Present.
